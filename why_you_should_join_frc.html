Why should you join FRC?

Are you on the fence about joining our FRC Team? Here are some reasons you should consider joining us.

What is FRC?

FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) is a robotics competition held in countries all around the globe. For the first 6 weeks of every year, teams design, manufacture, and program robots to complete the challenge given by FIRST. They then compete against each other for recognition, awards, and scholarships.

Now, lets get into why you should join us.

Our team gives you

Industry Experience

Joining an FRC team gives you a large amount of experience in many fields pertaining to engineering. During build season, when on the Mechanical / Design team, you learn how to safely use drills, saws, mills, and grinders. You learn how to prepare and test CAD Designs and how to manufacture them. You also gain skills in talking to vendors and manufacturers in order to meet what your team needs. On the Software / Electrical side, you gain unmatched experience in Java programming, which is one of the most widely used languages in Software Engineering. You learn how to write smart, efficient code, and how to properly use version control software, setting you leagues above the rest. On a grander scale, you can learn and experience what it’s like to be a leader, and how to manage projects.

At every FRC Tournament, there are always many adults from fields of engineering and science that help with volunteering, judging, or some that act as mentors, coaches, or sponsors. These tournaments are a great way to start networking, along with gaining general wisdom and knowledge that would be hard to get elsewhere.

Our team helps with

College

Being on an FRC Team gives you experience not many others have. When applying to colleges, this experience will set you a step above the rest, allowing you to shine out among the competition. This is especially helpful when applying to a school in the field of engineering.

Along with college applications, FRC gives you an opportunity to get scholarships nobody else can get. Each year FIRST gives out scholarships to students who participate in FIRST competitions. Regardless of if you have only been on a team for a year or for several, there are a plethora of scholarships available for you to get. There is a scholarship for everyone when on an FRC team.

On our team

There's Something For Everyone

FRC isn’t just for those interested in engineering. FIRST requires a diverse set of skills, including things such as Website Design and Development, Public Relations, and Graphic Design.

On our team, we have 3 separate teams that you can work for: Software / Electrical, Mechanical / Design, and Media / Publicity. On each team, you can learn wildly different skills that tailor to the field in which you work. These skills are as follows:

Mechanical / Design Creates the robot through a process of Research, Prototyping, Design, and Manufacturing.
Skills Include:

  • Understanding how to create CAD designs using Solidworks

  • Learning how to operate Mills and CNC routers

  • Assembling subsystems for the robot, along with assembling the robot itself

Software / Electrical Creates and programs the electrical systems that control the robot, manufactured by the Mechanical / Design team.
Skills Include:

  • Programming a robot using Java and WPILib

  • Properly using GitHub and Git for version control and management

  • Simulating a robot with Synthesis

  • Learning how to safely solder wires and joints together

  • Learning how to securely create wires and construct electrical systems

Media / Publicity Works on making our team look good for public view and possible sponsors and companies.
Skills Include:

  • Learning important outreach and social skills

  • Publicizing our team’s achievements and progress through social media and local television and newspapers

  • Taking photos and videos of our robot in use and the process of creating it

  • Designing posters and advertisements with Adobe Software such as Illustrator, Photoshop, InDesign, Lightroom, Premiere Pro, and After Effects

  • Scouting and communicating with other teams, mentors, and industry professionals at competitions

  • Maintaining a design aesthetic across all documents, flyers, posters, presentations, and social media posts

On our team, there is something for everyone regardless of interest.

On our team

You Will Have Fun!

Teams have spirit. Lots of it. Each and every one of us is proud of our creation, and we are all ready to show it through our clothing, our competition pit, and rooting for our robot. At competitions, stadiums are filled with festivity and excitement. People are cheering loudly for their teams, rooting for the drivers, and that their robot may come out at the top.

Along with this, during build season you bond with your team. For hours on end you are with each other working towards a common goal: making the best ing robot you can. We plan to have potlucks, barbecues, and events exclusive to FRC members, celebrating the hard work put into the season and competition. This hard work comes with challenges, but conquering these challenges fills you with motivation, drive, and excitement. During the process, you may learn things about yourself you never knew before, learn about interests you didn’t know you had, or learn skills you didn’t know you needed to know. FRC is filled with a world of opportunity, and it’s up to you - and your team - to find them.
