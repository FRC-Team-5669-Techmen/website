Team History

Team 5669 was founded in 2015 by Luis Garza, an alumnus and former teacher at Don Bosco Technical Institute. With a group of dedicated students, he entered the team in its rookie season at the Los Angeles Regional, where it finished 30th of 66, and built the program around a five-year plan to grow a new team into a regional playoff contender.

That plan paid off. The team improved steadily over the following seasons, reaching the playoffs at the 2018 Ventura Regional and posting its strongest run yet in 2019, when it ranked 8th of 49 at the Orange County Regional and again advanced to the playoffs.

The 2020 season ended early when FIRST suspended competition during the COVID-19 pandemic, and the disruption effectively reset the program. Much of the team's experience and momentum was lost, and 5669 found itself rebuilding almost from the ground up.

The years since have been a steady climb back. The team has grown stronger each season, regaining the depth and experience it lost, and California's move to a district model brought 5669 into the FIRST California District. In 2026 the rebuild reached a milestone: the team returned to the playoffs as a first-round alliance pick and posted its best performance in seven years.

School Info

Don Bosco Technical Institute is an all-male Catholic high school in Rosemead, California, focused on preparing the next generation of engineers and technologists. Students specialize in one of six technical pathways: Architecture & Construction Engineering (ACE); Biological, Medical, and Environmental Technology (BMET); Computer Science & Electrical Engineering (CSEE); Integrated Design, Engineering & Art (IDEA); Material Science, Engineering & Technology (MSET); and Media Arts & Technology (MAT).
